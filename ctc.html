Stand Up To Trash

Commit the Change at UCI

June 2023 - June 2024

Overview :Led a team of four UXUI Designers in creating and prototyping a web application to facilitate the transition from manual data collection on paper to efficient database-driven data collection.


Table of Contents

  • Design Question
  • How it Works
  • Final Products
  • Why it Matters

Design Question

How might we design a centralized way to streamline the process of volunteer registration and trash data collection?


Solution

Our solution was to make data collection efficient and create an external volunteer system to keep past volunteers recorded.




How it Works

Requirements Elicitation

First, we conducted a requirements elicitation, making sure we noted the wants, needs, and painpoints of the user and stakeholders, resulting in a very detailed Requirements Document.


User Persona

This persona is a depiction of the characteristics of target audience, based on real data collected during Requirements Elicitation.


Sketches

After understanding wants and needs, I created sketches & wireframes of what the event registration could look like.


Low-fi Wireframes

After a couple of sketches, we created low fidelity wireframes of what components could look like.


Hi-fi Wireframes

After recieving feedback from our NPO on our designs, we iterated on the components, finalizing the style and interaction.


Usability Testing

Once we had a working prototype, we conducted a usability test with our NPO, seeing what worked and what could be improved. This helped us create final iterations to our final product and prototype.



Final Products

Figma Prototype & Delivery

The final product was a Figma Prototype. This prototype has different walkthrough to show how the volunteer and admin can fully interact with it.

With this new product, we aim to reduce the organization's time on tracking and analyzing data from beach clean-up events by 30%.

The Prototype was accepted and delivered for Development, as our Developers began to build the software.
